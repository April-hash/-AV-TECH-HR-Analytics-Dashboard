# AV TECH HR Analytics Dashboard
## Description
This project presents an HR analytics dashboard developed in Power BI to analyze employee demographics, salary distribution, departmental structure, and company payouts. The dashboard helps visualize workforce composition, salary patterns, and employee compensation across different locations and departments.
## Project Overview
This project uses Power BI to analyze employee data from AV TECH. The dashboard provides insights into employee gender distribution, departmental workforce structure, salary trends, and company payout analysis across multiple locations. The goal is to support better HR decision-making and workforce planning.

## Key Business Questions Answered
### Employee Demographics
•	What is the gender distribution of employees in the organization?

•	How are employees distributed across departments and genders?

•	How are employees distributed across different locations?

•	How do employee ratings vary across genders?

### Salary and Compensation
•	What is the average salary by gender?

•	How does salary vary across departments?

•	How does salary differ across company locations?

•	What is the distribution of employees across salary bands?

### Payout and Location Analysis
•	What is the total company payout?

•	What is the total bonus percentage paid to employees?

•	What is the total bonus amount distributed?

•	Which locations have the highest employee payouts?

## Tools Used
•	Power BI

•	Power Query

## Key Findings
•	The company workforce consists of 946 employees across 12 departments.

•	Male employees represent the largest share of the workforce, followed by female employees.

•	Lagos, Kaduna, and Abuja are the three main employee locations.

•	Average salaries are relatively similar across genders, with only small differences observed.

•	Some departments such as Marketing, Accounting, and Training show higher average salaries compared to others.

•	The largest salary band contains the highest number of employees, indicating a concentration of mid-level earners.

•	Kaduna records the highest total payout, followed by Abuja and Lagos.

•	The company has a total payout of 71.92M, with 2.20M paid as bonuses.
